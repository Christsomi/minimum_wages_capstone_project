Youth Minimum Wage Hypotheses

Exploring Competing Perspectives on Minimum Wage Impacts

Labor Force Participation Rate

  • Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will decrease youth labor force participation by creating higher entry barriers

  • Contradictory Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will increase youth labor force participation by making work more attractive and financially viable

Employment/Population Ratio

  • Hypothesis

    Higher minimum wages will reduce the employment/population ratio for youth due to increased hiring selectivity

  • Contradictory Hypothesis

    Higher minimum wages will improve the employment/population ratio by incentivizing more youth to seek and maintain employment

Unemployment Rate

  • Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will elevate youth unemployment rates by reducing available entry-level positions

  • Contradictory Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will decrease youth unemployment by improving job quality and worker retention

Beginning of Quarter Employment: Counts

  • Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will decrease beginning of quarter employment counts for youth

  • Contradictory Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will stabilize or slightly increase beginning of quarter employment counts

Hires All: Counts

  • Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will significantly reduce total hire counts for young workers

  • Contradictory Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will maintain or slightly increase hire counts by attracting more committed workers

Separations: Counts

  • Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will reduce job separations due to increased job value and worker commitment

  • Contradictory Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will increase job separations as employers become more demanding and selective

Turnover (Stable)

  • Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will decrease job turnover by making existing jobs more valuable

  • Contradictory Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will increase job turnover as workers seek better-paying opportunities

Firm Job Gains: Counts

  • Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will reduce job creation, particularly in youth-intensive sectors

  • Contradictory Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will stimulate job gains by improving worker productivity and business efficiency

Firm Job Loss: Counts

  • Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will increase job losses for less productive young workers

  • Contradictory Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will reduce job losses by creating more stable employment environments

Firm Job Change: Net Change

  • Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will result in a negative net job change for youth employment

  • Contradictory Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will create a neutral or slightly positive net job change

Full Quarter Employment: Average Monthly Earnings

  • Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will moderately increase average monthly earnings for stable youth employment

  • Contradictory Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will have minimal impact on average monthly earnings due to reduced hours or employment

High School Dropout Rates

  • Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will reduce high school dropout rates by decreasing economic pressure to work

  • Contradictory Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases might increase dropout rates by making immediate work more financially attractive

High School Attendance

  • Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will improve high school attendance by reducing economic distractions

  • Contradictory Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases might negatively impact high school attendance by creating more appealing work opportunities

High School Enrollment Rates

  • Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will boost high school enrollment rates by making education more financially strategic

  • Contradictory Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases might slightly decrease high school enrollment rates by making immediate work more lucrative

College Enrollment Rates

  • Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will encourage higher college enrollment by highlighting the value of advanced education

  • Contradictory Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases might reduce college enrollment by making immediate work more financially appealing

High School Graduation Rates

  • Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases will improve high school graduation rates by reducing economic pressures

  • Contradictory Hypothesis

    Minimum wage increases might marginally impact graduation rates due to competing economic incentives
