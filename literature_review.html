Literature Review

Key research papers on minimum wage effects on youth employment and education

Minimum Wages and Employment: A Case Study of the Fast-Food Industry in New Jersey and Pennsylvania

David Card, Alan B. Krueger
1994

This seminal paper examines the impact of New Jersey's minimum wage increase on employment in fast-food restaurants, using Pennsylvania as a control group. The study found no evidence that the rise in New Jersey's minimum wage reduced employment at fast-food restaurants in the state.

The research challenged the conventional wisdom about the employment effects of minimum wage increases, sparking significant debate and further research in the field.

Method: Difference-in-Differences analysis using natural experiment design

Synthetic Control Methods for Comparative Case Studies

Alberto Abadie, Alexis Diamond, Jens Hainmueller
2010

This methodological paper introduces synthetic control methods for systematic analysis of policy interventions. The authors develop a framework that provides a data-driven approach to selecting comparison units in comparative case studies.

The method has become increasingly important in minimum wage research, allowing for more precise estimation of policy effects.

Method: Development of synthetic control methodology with applications

Minimum Wage Effects on Employment, Substitution, and the Teenage Labor Supply: Evidence from Personnel Data

Laura Giuliano
2013

This study uses personnel data from a large retail firm to examine the impact of minimum wage increases on employment and labor supply of teenage workers. The research finds significant effects on employment flows but limited impact on overall employment levels.

The paper provides unique insights using detailed individual-level employment data, offering a new perspective on minimum wage effects.

Method: Regression analysis using detailed personnel data with fixed effects
